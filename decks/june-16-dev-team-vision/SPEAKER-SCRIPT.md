# 🎤 Speaker Script — WeAssist 2036 Vision

**How to use this:** Just read it out loud, top to bottom. It's written the way you'd actually talk, not the way reports are written. Short lines. One idea at a time. Pause where you see a line break — those breaks ARE the pacing. The **bold** bits are where you lean in. You don't need to study this first. Read it cold and it'll carry you.

> `[CLICK]` = advance to the next slide. `(beat)` = pause one second. Let silences breathe — they make you sound confident, not nervous.

---

## SLIDE 0 — Title: "The 2036 Vision"

Okay. (beat)

So today isn't about what Dev did last month.

Today is about where this **whole company** is going — and the engine that actually gets us there.

Look at these three numbers. `[point at screen]`

One thousand seven hundred and fifty clients. Five thousand of our people placed in jobs. And a ratio of four to one.

Right now we're at **two-point-six-seven**. (beat)

So the question this whole deck answers is simple:

How do we get from where we are… to that?

`[CLICK]`

## SLIDE 1 — One mission. Felt everywhere.

Here's the thing that makes this matter.

Every single person we place… that's a family that eats. That's somebody's rent paid.

That's the mission. **Feed as many people as humanly possible.** Real jobs. Real opportunities.

And we're picky — less than two percent of people who apply actually get in.

We're not a staffing agency that throws bodies at a problem. We run a **90-day program** that sets people up to actually win.

So this vision? It's not Dev's plan. (beat) It's everybody's. Because the mission has to be felt in **every** room, not just where the code gets written.

`[CLICK]`

---

## SLIDE 2 — The number that frames everything.

Now let me show you what that goal actually demands of us.

To hit 1,750 clients and 5,000 people by 2036…

we have to grow about **forty-five percent every year.** Clients and people both.

And that ratio — it has to climb from 2.67 up to **four.**

Here's why that's the whole game: (beat)

You do **not** get there by hiring more and more people. That doesn't scale. You'd drown.

You get there by making **each person able to carry more** — and the only way to do that is with systems. With automation.

That's it. That's the entire strategy on one slide.

`[CLICK]`

---

## SLIDE 3 — The honest starting line.

Okay, now I'm going to be **really** honest with you. The kind of honest most companies won't say out loud. (beat)

We tell the world we keep 98% of our clients.

The real number? We're losing about **twenty-two percent a month.**

`[let that sit for a second]`

What does that actually mean? It means our entire client base basically rebuilds itself every **four and a half months.** We're refilling a leaky bucket.

And every time a client leaves and we have to replace them, we burn that **$2,500** it costs to land a new one. One client lost takes about **three new ones** just to break even.

We've got **326 written-down reasons** people canceled. We read them. We're not hiding from them.

So here's the goal: take that 22%… and drive it down to **five.** (beat)

And the lever that does it is the 90-day program. Fix retention, and **everything** else gets easier.

`[CLICK]`

## SLIDE 4 — What every team looks like at 5,000 people.

So we asked every department one question: (beat)

"What do **you** look like when we're at 5,000 people?"

And every answer came back needing the same two things underneath it. Watch — `[gesture across the cards]`

Recruitment: candidates sorted automatically, and matched to clients by a real score — not a gut guess. We call that engine **Argus.**

After-Sales: the 90-day reports build themselves, and an account that's about to leave gets **flagged before it leaves.**

Sales: not just sourcing fees anymore — premium diagnostics, courses, departments that **earn their own keep.**

Marketing: one real presence online that actually shows what we do.

HR and Finance: the scorecard calculates itself. No more hand-assembling spreadsheets at midnight.

And Dev and AI: **one AI-capable person inside every department.** The glue holding all of it together.

See the pattern? (beat) Every single team needs **clean data and automation.**

That's the moment Dev's work stops being a Dev story… and becomes the **company's** story.

`[CLICK]`

## SLIDE 5 — Small core. AI everywhere.

Now here's the part people don't expect.

To run all of that… Dev stays **tiny.** Four to six core engineers. That's it.

We don't grow by hiring an army. (beat) We grow by **automation.**

But — every department gets at least one AI-capable person that **we** trained and **we** support. Spread out, embedded, everywhere.

And once we cross 50-plus clients, Dev itself splits into specialists — this is **KP's** plan: QA testing, full-stack, an AI specialist, and a business analyst.

Small core. AI everywhere. That's how a handful of people run a company of thousands.

`[CLICK]`

## SLIDE 6 — From cost center to product.

Okay, this is my favorite part. (beat)

Right now, Dev looks like a cost. We're the team that spends money to build things.

Here's the flip. `[lean in]`

Everything we build to make **WeAssist** run… is the exact same thing we can **sell** to every other company like us.

It's called **WorkStack OS** — basically an operating system for running offshore teams. And WeAssist is its first and **toughest** customer.

Think about it: if our automation can make **us** run at a 4-to-1 ratio… that's the proof that makes it sellable to everyone else.

Two more engines push it further: **agentic recruitment** to slash hiring time, and an **AI agent marketplace** so the people we place get their work done faster.

So the tools we build just to survive… (beat) become the products that make us money.

Cost center… to product.

`[CLICK]`

## SLIDE 7 — What we build now.

So that's the dream. Here's what's actually being built **right now.** Not someday — now.

One: get the whole company onto **one single database.** Our own, on our own servers. Instead of a hundred scattered spreadsheets that don't talk to each other.

Two: **Nexus v2** — the friendly, AI-forward screen everybody actually uses.

Three: **Autobots v2** — pulls our meeting transcription **off** the expensive Amazon bill and onto our own setup.

Four: a **ticketing loop.** A metric goes off-track, it automatically becomes a ticket, it gets an owner, it's fixed within a week. Nothing slips through the cracks.

And five: **zero reports built by hand.** Ever again.

Because at 5,000 people, clean data isn't a nice-to-have. (beat) It's the **only** thing that makes that size survivable.

`[CLICK]`

Now, I don't want you to think this is all theory and big dreams. (beat)

We've **already** found the money.

Over **fifty-two thousand dollars a year** in savings. Identified. Mapped out. Real.

And here's the best part — it's all invisible **backend** stuff. None of it touches the tools the team actually loves using. It just… drops straight to the bottom line.

Autobots v2 kills the Amazon and Railway usage bills. Our AI tooling moved to Gemini — six hundred bucks a month, handled. And there's another **$3,600 a month** under review right now.

Every dollar we save, every hour we automate — it's the **same lever** as that 4-to-1 ratio. (beat) More impact, per person.

## SLIDE 9 — Plan first. Then auto-populate.

So how do we actually steer all this? (beat)

We pick **15 numbers.** That's it. The 15 metrics that tell us if the company is healthy.

Every week, you scan them in **five minutes.** On-track, or off-track.

Anything off-track automatically becomes an issue we deal with. A closed loop. Nothing gets ignored.

But — and this matters — we **plan** those 15 numbers **before** we build anything. Because once it's wired to live data, that's literally what the company runs on. You don't wing that part.

**Grace and Eric** lock the targets by **June 29th.** Then KP and I build the scorecard so it fills **itself** in, every week, automatically.

## 🫁 Quick reminders before you walk up

- **Breathe at every line break.** The white space is your friend — it's not dead air, it's confidence.
- **The numbers are your anchors.** 1,750 · 5,000 · 4.0 · 22% → 5% · $52K. If you blank, grab a number and the story comes back.
- **Slide 3 (the 22% truth) is the heart.** Slow down there. That honesty is what makes the whole room trust everything else you say.
- **Slide 6 (cost center → product) is your peak.** That's where you sound like a CTO. Let your energy spike.
- You don't need to memorize this. You need to **read it like you mean it** — and it's written so that reading it *makes* you mean it.
